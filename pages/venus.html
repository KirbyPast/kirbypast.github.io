Venus

1. General Information

  • Venus also known as the Light Moon is the second planet from the Sun. It is named after the Roman goddess of love and beauty . It is the third brightest natural object in the night sky after the Sun and the Moon . Its observed stellar magnitude reaches -4.6 m and is sufficient for reflected light from Venus to create shadows; it is rarely visible to the naked eye during the day.

  • Venus is a telluric planet and is sometimes called Earth's "sister planet" due to their similar size, mass, proximity to the Sun, and chemical composition. In other respects it differs radically from Terra. It has the densest atmosphere of the four telluric planets, consisting of more than 96% carbon dioxide . Atmospheric pressure at the planet's surface is 92 times that on Earth, or about the pressure found 900 m underwater on Earth. Venus is by far the hottest planet in the Solar System, with an average surface temperature of 735 K (462 °C ; 863 °F), even though Mercury is closer to the Sun. 

    2.Atmosphere and climate

  • Venus has an extremely dense atmosphere composed of 96.5% carbon dioxide , 3.5% nitrogen , and traces of other gases, including sulfur dioxide . The mass of its atmosphere is 93 times that of Earth, while the pressure at its surface is about 92 times that of Earth—a pressure equivalent to that at a depth of nearly 1 km under the oceans Earth ui. The density at the surface is 65 kg/m 3 , or 50 times denser than the Earth's atmosphere at 293 K (20 °C) at sea level.

  • The large amount of CO 2 generates the strongest greenhouse effect in the Solar System, creating surface temperatures of at least 735 K (462 °C).This makes the surface of Venus hotter than that of Mercury , which has a minimum surface temperature of 53 K (−220 °C) and a maximum surface temperature of 700 K (427 °C),even though Venus is almost twice as far from the Sun as Mercury and receives only 25% of Mercury's solar irradiance. This temperature is higher than that used for sterilization.

  • Venus's atmosphere is extremely enriched in primordial noble gases compared to Earth's.This enrichment indicates an early divergence from the evolving Earth. An unusually large impact with a comet or accretion of a more massive primary atmosphere from the solar nebula have been proposed to explain the enrichment. 

    3.Surface geology

  • Much of the Venusian surface appears to have been shaped by volcanic activity. Venus has more volcanoes than Earth, 167 of which are large volcanoes that are over 100 km in diameter. The only volcanic complex of this size on Earth is the Big Island of Hawaii. This is not because Venus has more active volcanic activity than Earth, but because its crust is older.

  • Earth's oceanic crust is continuously recycled by subduction at tectonic plate boundaries and has an average age of about 100 million years old, while the Venusian surface is estimated to be 300–600 million years old. 

    4.Observations

  • To the naked eye, Venus appears as a white point of light brighter than any other planet or star (apart from the Sun ).The mean apparent magnitude of the planet is −4.14 with a standard deviation of 0.31. Brightest magnitude occurs in the crescent phase about a month before or after inferior conjunction. Venus dims to magnitude −3 when backlit by the Sun.The planet is bright enough to be seen in a clear midday sky, and is more easily visible when the Sun is low on the horizon or at sunset. It never moves away from the Sun more than about 47°.

  • Venus "passes" Earth every 584 days as it orbits the Sun.As it does so, it changes from the "Evening Glow", visible after sunset, to the "Morning Glow", visible before sunrise. Although Mercury reaches a maximum elongation (declination from the Sun) of only 28° and is often difficult to see in twilight, Venus is hard to miss when it is at its brightest. Its significant maximum elongation means that it is visible in dark skies long after sunset. As the brightest object in the sky, Venus is often interpreted by non-astronomers as an " unidentified flying object ".
